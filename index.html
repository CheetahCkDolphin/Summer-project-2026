NSDA Unified Rules Summary

Original Oratory (OO)

A persuasive speech written by the student that attempts to convince or inspire. Maximum duration is 10 minutes, with a 30-second grace period. Exceeding 10:30 prevents a first-place rank. No more than 150 words of direct quotations are allowed in the entire speech.

Informative Speaking (INF)

An informative speech written by the student designed to explain or describe a topic. Maximum duration is 10 minutes, with a 30-second grace period. Audio analysis is identical to Oratory, focusing on clarity, WPM pacing, and structural signposts.

Extemporaneous Speaking (USX/IX)

Students draw a question on current events, prepare for 30 minutes, and speak for a maximum of 7 minutes (with a 30-second grace period). Key rules include strict time constraints (7:30 maximum) and heavy reliance on verbal source citations (e.g. "According to the New York Times of June 12th...").

Impromptu Speaking (IMP)

Contestants have a total of 7 minutes for both preparation and speaking (with a 30-second grace period). A common approach is 2 minutes of prep and 5 minutes of delivery. Pacing is critical to fit the entire topic analysis in the brief speaking window.

Interpretation (DI/HI)

Performances of published literature. Maximum duration is 10 minutes (30s grace). Audio focus is heavily on character vocal distinctions, dramatic pacing, emotional pauses, and articulation.
